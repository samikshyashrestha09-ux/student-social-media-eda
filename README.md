# Student Social Media & Mental Health — EDA

## About the Project
Exploratory Data Analysis on how social media usage impacts the 
mental health, sleep, and academic habits of students.

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle
- 5000 rows, 13 columns
- Includes demographic, social media behavior, lifestyle and mental health data

## Key Findings
- Higher average daily social media usage is strongly associated with lower mental health scores (-0.82 correlation)
- Stress levels are inversely correlated with both sleep hours and mental health — students with very high stress sleep only 5-6 hours per night
- Increased study hours and physical activity positively impact mental health scores

## Libraries Used
- Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn
